AMVETS

Check it out here:

WEBSITE

PROTOTYPE

Type:

Individual Project

Role:

Wireframing, Prototyping, UI/UX Design

Tools:

Figma

Duration:

2 days

You know you're a legitimate designer when an organization reaches out to you for an opportunity to work. When America's biggest veterans non-profit asked me to design their donation check out page, I felt the weight of responsibility on my shoulder like never before. If someone decided to hold their donations because of my work, it would be hard to live it down.

Greyscale Wireframe

My first task was to conceptualize how the donation process would work. I opted for 2 windows: the first would display the donation options, and the second would display the payment method. I chose this design to reduce scrolling. Scrolling would add tedium for the user if they decided to go back and make changes to their donation.

High-Fidelity Prototpe

The high fidelity prototype built ontop of the idea of 2 screens. I was instructed to add a dedication form to the process if the user chose to opt into dedicating their donation to a loved one. I integrated it by adding a radio button that triggered a slide animation, overlapping the form with the donation page. My client liked the solution but advised against the proposed visual layout as it was too distinct from AMVETS's visual identity.

Final Product

After the donation page was finished, I was delighted to see that the AMVETS team had built on top of my design. They removed the tabs on the website and placed the additional donation rewareds on the same section as the dollar donation, streamlining navigation further. They also iterated on check-out info, tallying them up neatly. Even though this was a team effort, I was given credit for providing the developers with a good base to add the new features in the final product.

Reflection

Despite only being a cog in the machine, I was elated to be part of this project. The COO of AMVETS had personally contacted me afterwards and offered future opportunities for collaboration. Obviously, I accepted with a great deal of gratitudeThis was the kind of project that made me feel proud to be in the vocation i chose.
